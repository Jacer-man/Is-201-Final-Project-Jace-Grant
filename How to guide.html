Ride Level: Beginner

Dial In Your Foundation

Before you unlock the flashier moves, this guide focuses on building effortless control, smooth balance, and clean riding technique. Master these fundamentals and every trick becomes easier.

Safety Checklist
  • Always wear a helmet, wrist guards, and pads.
  • Practice on smooth, open pavement away from cars & crowds.
  • Keep your speed low while learning — style comes before speed.
  • Stop if you feel fatigued; tired legs = sloppy balance.
Trick 01

Confident Mount & Dismount

Level 1 · Essential Control

Before any flashy tricks, you need smooth, repeatable mounts and dismounts. Think of this as your “clean start and stop” combo.

Steps
  • Place the board on flat ground with the nose pointing forward.
  • Stand beside the board, shoulders squared, knees slightly bent.
  • Place your front foot fully on the front pad, centered and level.
  • Slowly transfer weight onto the front foot as your back foot steps on.
  • Engage your core, look where you want to go, and gently lean forward.
  • To dismount, slow down, bring the board almost to a stop, then lift your front heel off the pad or use your board’s simple-stop mode.
  • Step off with your back foot first, then your front foot, staying low and balanced.

Repeat this 10–15 times on each side until mounting and dismounting feel boring.

Trick 02

Basic Carves & S-Turns

Level 2 · Flow Builder

Smooth carving is what makes Onewheel riding feel like snowboarding on pavement. These S-turns also teach you fine control over your edges.

Steps
  • Start rolling at a slow, comfortable speed on a wide, empty stretch of pavement.
  • Keep your knees soft and your weight centered over the wheel.
  • To initiate a toe-side carve, gently lean your shins and knees toward your toes.
  • As the board turns, keep your chest facing forward and look through the turn.
  • To switch to heel-side, smoothly roll your weight back toward your heels.
  • Link turns together: toe → heel → toe, making long, lazy S-shapes down your practice area.
  • Focus on staying relaxed and quiet with your upper body — all the motion comes from your ankles and knees.

Aim for long, wide turns at first; as you gain control, you can make them tighter and more dynamic.

Trick 03

Ride Switch (Fakie)

Level 3 · Balance Upgrade

Riding switch (your non-dominant stance forward) doubles your control and makes every future trick easier. Go slow and stay patient.

Steps
  • From a normal stance, slow down until you’re barely rolling.
  • Step off, rotate the board 180°, and remount with your opposite foot forward.
  • Start with tiny, straight-line rolls in switch — 10–15 feet is enough.
  • Keep your shoulders relaxed and your gaze fixed far ahead, not at your feet.
  • Use micro-adjustments in your ankles to keep the board centered under you.
  • As you gain control, add very gentle toe/heel leans to introduce small carves.
  • Practice alternating sets: one run regular, one run switch, back and forth.

Add switch practice to every session. Even 5 minutes per ride will make a huge difference over time.

Trick 04

Small Curb Drops

Level 4 · Street Skill

Learning controlled drops opens up city riding and basic urban lines. Start tiny, nail the technique, then slowly work bigger.

Steps
  • Find a very small drop first (like a driveway lip or 1–2" curb).
  • Approach straight on at walking speed, knees bent and eyes forward.
  • Right before the edge, shift your hips slightly back to keep the nose from diving.
  • Stay loose in your knees and ankles to absorb the landing.
  • Let the wheel roll out after the drop; don’t slam your weight forward.
  • Repeat on the tiny drop until it feels smooth, then step up to a normal curb.
  • Only increase speed once you can land consistently without nose-dive or wobble.

If you’re nervous, practice off a painted line or crack first, pretending it’s a drop to get the timing and weight shift dialed.
